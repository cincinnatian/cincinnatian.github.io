Cultural Blog - Week5

Pairing was at times fun and helpful and at other times not so much. I think that there is enough variability in the ways people code that at times trying to view someone else's code becomes somewhat of a chore if they followed a completely different approach. I would concede that it probably does make you learn the material better. I enjoyed the coding sessions I have had thus far but usually have a hard time knowing whether or not I'm impinging on someone else's desire to drive. Even when asking I don't know for sure that that is what they want as I have read my feedback. It was difficult to give negative feedback to my pair because I don't know their background enough to tell them how they can improve or to know why they made the mistakes that they made or really if their mistakes weren't mistakes at all but just a thought process they were going through. This is why these feedback mechanisms don't seem to be totally useful to me yet.
